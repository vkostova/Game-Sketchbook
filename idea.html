THE IDEA

The game I want to create is about wastefulness and how ones actions can influence your environment. Currently, I am thinking about distribution of food supplies and how they can be wasted or not distributed equally. I want the player to have a growing population that needs to be taken care of but problems will arise with the increasing amount of people and the resources that are needed to take care of everybody. I would like the player to have a hard time deciding what choice to make. Like the game "Papers, Please!" I want the player to make difficult decisions which do not promise a good outcome every time.

THE INSPIRATION

As mentioned before I find inspiration in the game Papers, Please!. The difficult choices made within the game are something I want to achieve as well. The interactive short movie and game by Michael Frei, a Swiss filmmaker and artist, is one of the inspirations for the game that is going to be developed. The minimalistic design and interactivity are what I find interesting and would like to take into considerations for my prototypes. Flow is a game about growing by collecting. I find this mechanic interesting because I am playing with the idea of using it for my game by growing the number of people by collecting them during the game.

The aesthetic that I found a liking to was isometric so I had to think about how to get away from a flat design and into a more three-dimensional design. Somewhere in between. 2.5.

So I started experimenting with unity and viewpoints.

Try it!

The aesthetic goal for this game is meant to be very simple. I want the player to be able to focus and fully experience the main mechanic of the game without being distracted by non-essential elements.

The beginning of the game the environment will be as empty as possible. Only the player character and interactive elements like the resources will be visible. As the player progresses through the level I want the environment to fill more and more but again only with a growing population (clones of the main character) and growing resources (clones of resources). The whole game will be build with these two main elements. I am considering to change the color of the different resources in order to introduce more colors into the game.

I am hoping to create a focused vision with these elements and allow the players to understand how the mechanic works through a playful way, because I want to refrain from giving any tutorials or explanations. The player will be thrown into the game without knowing anything but due to the lack of elements (at least in the beginning) I want to allow for a slow process of understanding.
